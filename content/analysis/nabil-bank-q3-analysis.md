  ## Company Overview

  Nabil Bank Limited (NABIL) is Nepal's largest commercial bank by net profit and one of the oldest private-sector banks in the country. Listed on the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE) since November 24, 1985, Nabil has a total paid-up capital of Rs. 100 per share with 270,569,984 listed shares outstanding, giving it a market capitalization of approximately NPR 1.42 Kharab (Rs. 142.59 billion) as of mid-May 2026. The bank operates one of the widest branch networks in Nepal and has been a consistent top performer in the banking sector for multiple fiscal years.

  ## Q3 FY 2082/83 Financial Performance

  Nabil Bank delivered an exceptional third-quarter report for FY 2082/83, reinforcing its position as Nepal's most profitable commercial bank. The headline numbers are impressive: net profit rose by 33.92% year-over-year to reach Rs. 6.07 Arba (approximately Rs. 6.07 billion) through Falgun of the fiscal year. This profit figure places Nabil at the very top of the commercial banking sector profit chart, surpassing all other A-class banks including NMB Bank, Global IME Bank, and NIC Asia Bank.

  ### Revenue and Profit Breakdown

  The bank's total operating income showed meaningful growth driven by both net interest income and non-interest income. Net interest income benefited from a gradual improvement in the net interest margin as the bank repriced its loan portfolio upward in response to NRB's monetary policy adjustments. On the cost side, credit impairment charges remained manageable, reflecting a controlled non-performing loan (NPL) ratio that has been trending downward.

  **Fee Income:** One of the standout metrics in Q3 was the 26% growth in fee and commission income. This signals strong momentum in transactional banking services including remittances, trade finance, digital banking, and card services. For a bank of Nabil's scale, double-digit fee income growth indicates successful diversification beyond traditional lending — a critical quality factor for long-term sustainability.

  ## Valuation Analysis

  At a market price of Rs. 527, Nabil Bank trades at a moderate valuation relative to its earnings growth trajectory. The bank's price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio is broadly in line with the commercial banking sector average, which typically ranges between 12x to 18x for large-cap banks. However, Nabil's superior profit growth rate of nearly 34% suggests the stock may still offer reasonable value for growth-oriented investors compared to peers growing at single-digit rates.

  The book value per share provides another useful lens. With a paid-up value of Rs. 100 and accumulated reserves, Nabil trades at a price-to-book (P/B) ratio that reflects market confidence in its asset quality and earnings capacity. For income-focused investors, the historical dividend yield of approximately 1.4% (based on Rs. 7.50 per share) is modest but supplemented by the potential for capital appreciation as earnings continue to grow.
