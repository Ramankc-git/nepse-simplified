## What is Technical Analysis?

Technical analysis is the study of historical price and volume data to identify patterns and trends that can help predict future price movements. Unlike fundamental analysis, which focuses on a company's financial statements and business prospects, technical analysis focuses purely on the price action as displayed on charts. The underlying philosophy is that all known information about a company is already reflected in its stock price, and that prices tend to move in trends that repeat over time due to the collective psychology of market participants.

For Nepali stock market investors, technical analysis is particularly relevant because the NEPSE market exhibits strong trending behavior. When a stock starts moving in a direction (up or down), it tends to continue in that direction for an extended period before reversing.

> **Technical analysis does not predict the future with certainty.** It provides probabilities, not guarantees. The goal is to identify setups where the odds of success are in your favor and manage risk appropriately when the analysis proves wrong.

## Support and Resistance

Support and resistance are the most fundamental concepts in technical analysis. Support is a price level where buying interest becomes strong enough to prevent the price from declining further. It acts as a floor beneath the price. Resistance is a price level where selling pressure becomes strong enough to prevent the price from rising further. It acts as a ceiling above the price.

On NEPSE, support and resistance levels often form at round numbers (like NPR 1,000, 1,200, 1,500) because many investors place buy and sell orders at these psychological price points.

- Support levels indicate areas where buyers have historically stepped in. Look for increased volume and bullish candlestick patterns near support.
- Resistance levels indicate areas where sellers have historically taken profits. Look for selling pressure and bearish candlestick patterns near resistance.
- When a support level breaks (price closes below it), it often becomes new resistance. When resistance breaks, it often becomes new support.
- The strength of a support or resistance level increases with each test.
- Volume is critical. A breakout on high volume is more likely to be genuine.

## Trend Lines

A trend line is a straight line drawn on a chart that connects a series of price points to visually represent the direction and speed of a price trend. An uptrend line is drawn by connecting two or more consecutive higher lows, while a downtrend line connects two or more consecutive lower highs.

To draw a valid trend line, you need at least two points of contact, but three or more points make the trend line significantly more reliable. When a well-established trend line is broken, it signals a potential trend reversal or a transition to a consolidation phase.

## Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out daily price fluctuations to reveal the underlying trend direction. The two most commonly used moving averages in the Nepali market are the Simple Moving Average (SMA) and the Exponential Moving Average (EMA).

- **50-day MA:** Medium-term trend indicator
- **200-day MA:** Long-term trend indicator
- **20-day EMA:** Short-term momentum signal

A common trading signal is generated when a shorter-term moving average crosses above or below a longer-term moving average. When the 50-day MA crosses above the 200-day MA, it is called a Golden Cross (bullish). When the 50-day MA crosses below the 200-day MA, it is called a Death Cross (bearish).

## Key Chart Patterns

### Head and Shoulders

The Head and Shoulders pattern is one of the most reliable reversal patterns. It consists of three peaks: a higher middle peak (the head) flanked by two lower peaks (the shoulders). The pattern forms after an uptrend and signals a potential reversal to a downtrend.

### Double Top and Double Bottom

A Double Top forms when a stock tests the same resistance level twice and fails to break through, signaling exhaustion of buying momentum. A Double Bottom forms when a stock tests the same support level twice and holds, signaling a potential reversal to the upside.

### Triangles

Triangle patterns form when the price range narrows over time. Ascending triangles typically resolve with a breakout to the upside. Descending triangles typically resolve with a breakdown to the downside. Symmetrical triangles can break either way. Triangle patterns are common on NEPSE during consolidation phases.

## Volume Analysis

Volume is the number of shares traded during a given period and is an essential confirmation tool. In a healthy uptrend, volume should increase on up days and decrease on down days. When price moves occur on unusually high volume, it indicates strong conviction from market participants.

On NEPSE, a stock breaking above resistance on volume that is 2-3 times its 30-day average is a strong bullish signal.

> **Tip:** Begin with the daily chart of a single NEPSE stock you own. Identify the most recent support and resistance levels, draw a trend line connecting the recent swing points, and overlay the 20-day and 50-day moving averages. Spend two weeks just observing how price interacts with these levels before making any trading decisions.

## Conclusion

Technical analysis is a skill that develops with practice and observation. Start with the core concepts of support, resistance, and trend identification before moving on to more complex patterns and indicators. The most successful technical analysts on NEPSE combine chart analysis with an understanding of market sentiment, liquidity conditions, and macroeconomic factors.
